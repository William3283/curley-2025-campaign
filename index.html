About J.A. Curley

J.A. Curley has spent his career fighting for the principles of liberty, fiscal responsibility, and national security. From serving as an economic adviser to presidents to his tenure as Governor of South Carolina, Curley has the experience and vision to lead America forward.

Candidate Positions on Issues

Foreign Policy

J.A. Curley believes that the United States should be the champion of democracy and freedom around the world. As President, Curley will:

  • Defend the ideals of democracy and freedom wherever they are threatened.
  • Act alone to promote the country’s national interests when necessary.
  • Move the UN headquarters from New York City to Switzerland to promote neutrality and reduce bureaucracy.

Crime and Punishment

J.A. Curley supports a zero-tolerance policy for criminal behavior. He believes the United States should:

  • Increase penalties for crimes because punishment is the best deterrent.
  • Expand mandatory minimum sentencing laws.
  • Apply the death penalty automatically to felonies involving drug trafficking and terrorism.
  • Remove any possibility of parole for criminals who commit violent felonies.

Environment

J.A. Curley believes that our natural resources should be used in ways that are most beneficial to all. As President, Curley will:

  • Privatize national parks so that companies can efficiently manage them and determine the best use for the land.
  • Recognize that global warming is a theory perpetuated by alarmists and will not provide federal funding for research into alternative fuels.
  • Oppose any laws that would require businesses to adopt costly environmentally friendly policies.

Social Services for the Poor

J.A. Curley believes the government should rely on private businesses and faith-based charities to provide social services. He supports the following actions:

  • End government-subsidized health care programs like Medicare and Medicaid.
  • Turn Medicare and Medicaid programs over to private health insurance providers to decrease the cost of administering such programs.
  • End all federally funded welfare programs, as it is not the government’s responsibility to provide social services.
  • Provide full federal funding for faith-based social service agencies, which can then administer social services to the needy.

Talking Points

A Stronger America Through Law and Order

Curley’s policies will ensure that criminals face swift justice and make America a safer place for all.

Reclaiming Our Resources for Economic Prosperity

Curley supports privatizing national parks and unlocking resources for responsible economic growth.

Ending Government Overreach

Curley will reduce government spending and open federal services to private-sector competition, ensuring a more efficient and accountable government.
